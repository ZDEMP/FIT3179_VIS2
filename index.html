Visualisation 2 - US Election Data Analysis

This Visualisation explains data from the US elections over the last 30 years. Below you will see a comparison between the voting breakdown of the 1976 election and the 2016 election, notice the visual change in party preferences. The darker the colour, the "more" Republican or Democrat the State is, ie. higher winning percentage.


Analysis of States Data

Comparing the two maps above, the change in voting patterns between 1976 and 2016 is quite significant, major changes in States like California and Alabama have really shown the way people from each state changes over the years. However, there are some states that have stayed with there Republican or Democrat views, such as Nebraska and Maryland.

The interactive bubble plot to the right displays the relationship between a states population and the amount of votes, obviously we would expect as the population increases, the number of voters increase, which is mostly true. However it is interesting use the filter of votes per 100 people to see which states have higher votes per capita then others (Maine being the highest).

Republican - Democrat and Independent Analysis

The main two popular parties in the US are the Republicans and the Democrats. However, the coverage on the independent statistics is often under-represented, the following visuals covers all three categories.

The visual on the left uses normalised data to show the trend over the years purely between Democrats and Republican. As you can see, the trend is that the total votes for the two parties have been almost 50/50 since 1992. The visual on the right shows the two main parties combined against the Independents. With an overall upwards tick in votes of the years, the proportion of independent voters in 2016 was the third highest behind 1992 and 1996. This could be something we see rising this year.
